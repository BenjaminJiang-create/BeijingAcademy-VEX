What is VEX?

VEX Robotics is a global, game-based robotics program that connects students with real engineering and teamwork.

VEX Robotics provides a series of hands-on robotics programs where students design, build, and program robots to play a new game every season. From the first sketch to the final match, teams experience the full engineering design process: brainstorming, prototyping, testing, and iteration.

VEX is not only a competition; it is a platform that encourages creativity, resilience, and leadership. Students learn to treat every failure as data, and every match as feedback for the next improvement.

Why VEX at Beijing Academy?

At Beijing Academy, we want robotics to be more than a competition team—we want it to be a learning environment where students can connect classroom theory with real-world engineering practice.

  • Project-based learning: VEX gives students a concrete project that integrates physics, math, computer science, and design.
  • Student leadership: Teams are student-run. Members take responsibility for planning, documenting, communicating, and making technical decisions.
  • Inclusive opportunities: VEX offers roles for builders, programmers, drivers, notebook writers, media creators, and strategists—students with different strengths can all contribute.
  • Pathways to competitions and outreach: Through local and regional events, students gain experience presenting their work, collaborating with other schools, and representing Beijing Academy in the wider STEM community.

By building a strong VEX program, we hope to create a long-term platform where students can grow from beginners to mentors, and where each season’s experience is passed on to the next generation of builders and innovators.
